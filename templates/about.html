About

What is D&D?

D&D (Dungeons and Dragons) in the 1970s. Wargaming enthusiast Gary Gygax took inspiration from Tolkien, Wargames and mythology to create a board-game where you would move pewter adventurers through dangerous mazes filled with monsters and traps in search of treasure. Elements of roleplaying, adding personality and characters to your pewter-figurine grew more and more important as the franchise grew.
Fans of D&D like both the board-gaming with friends aspect as well as the creative outlet of what is essentially a form of improv-theater. In 2020, with all the stress of social-distancing in much of the world, community and friendships matter a great deal in maintaining health not just physically but mentally as well. D&D is not just pewter and improv - It's an excuse for you and your friends to get together on voice-chat and video once a week and participate in leisure together.

Where can I learn to play D&D?

Good question! D&D has been around for a very long time, so chances are even if you've never played it - someone in your area is running a game! Ask around with your friends if they'd like to try out a one-off adventure module (Many are available for purchase, pre-written, on sites such as Drivethru-RPG). If you're feeling shy, there's plenty of D&D-gaming available online! Sites such as Roll20, with an advanced web-GUI that allows you to play the game online, on a tiled map, with streamlined rules that makes it easier than ever to learn.
The barrier to entry is not very high, so visit Roll20 and sign up to see if you can't find a game that suits you!
